COMMITTEES

United Nations General Assembly (Plenary Session) (UNGA-Plenary)

The General Assembly Plenary considers issues that are best addressed comprehensively or that require coordinating work between many bodies of the United Nations. The UN General Assembly (UNGA) is the main policy-making organ of the Organization. Comprising all Member States, it provides a unique forum for multilateral discussion of the full spectrum of international issues covered by the Charter of the United Nations.

Agenda

Deliberation upon the reformation proposals of the United Nations with emphasis upon the structure of the Security Council

Conference of Parties (COP27)

A conference of the parties is the supreme governing body of an international convention. It is composed of representatives of the member states of the convention and accredited observers. The scope of the COP is to review the "implementation of the Convention and any other legal instruments that the COP adopts and take decisions necessary to promote the effective implementation of the Convention"

Agenda

Addressing and ensuring accountability for climate action with special emphasis upon reviewing the implementation of the Paris Agreement

International Law Commission (ILC)

The International Law Commission is a body of experts responsible for helping develop and codify international law. It is composed of 34 individuals recognized for their expertise and qualifications in international law, who are elected by the UNGA every five years. The ILC has since held annual sessions at the UN Office in Geneva to discuss and debate various topics in international law and develop international legal principles accordingly.

Agenda

Questioning the legality and acceptability of Foreign Intervention with special emphasis upon "Responsibility To Protect" (R2P)

Parliamentary Standing Committee on External Affairs (PSCEA)

The Committee on External Affairs is one of the 16 Departmentally related Standing Committees of the Lok Sabha. Each DRSC consists of 31 Members – 21 from Lok Sabha and 10 from Rajya Sabha. The Chairman of the Standing Committee on External Affairs is appointed by the Speaker, Lok Sabha from amongst Members of the Committee. A Minister is not eligible to be nominated as a Member of any of the Standing Committees.

Agenda

Discussion upon the changing dynamics of the Indian Foreign Policy with emphasis on the changing world order

World Health Assembly (WHA)

The World Health Assembly, is a subsidiary of the World Health Organization is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. The WHO Constitution states its main objective as "the attainment by all peoples of the highest possible level of health". Headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland, it has six regional offices and 150 field offices worldwide.

Agenda

Achieving Universal Health Coverage (UHC) by 2030 with emphasis on advanced healthcare for all.

Age Restriction

Only for students of classes 6th to 8th.

International Press Corps (IPC)

The International Press Delegation allows participants to act as unbiased journalists, reporting on the proceedings of each simulation of the conference. Reporters are responsible for observing the simulations as they unfold and disseminating information regarding those events to all of the participants. In doing so, Reporters are responsible for conducting their background research, whether it be gathering basic information on relevant topics or conducting interviews.

Members

Reporters, Cartoonists, and Photographers
